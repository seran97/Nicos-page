Buying Guide: 3 Steps to the Right Dashcam

Decide How Many Channels You Need

A single-channel dashcam only records the front. A 2-channel covers front and rear. A 3-channel dashcam like the Galphi also covers the interior — essential for rideshare drivers, parents monitoring teen drivers, or anyone wanting full 360° documentation. If you drive for Uber, Lyft, or any rideshare service, a 3-channel setup is the smart choice at this price.

Check Resolution & Night Vision

Front camera resolution matters most — 2.5K captures license plates clearly in daylight. For night driving, look for cameras with f/1.8 or wider apertures and infrared LEDs for interior recording. The Galphi's 2.5K front lens handles most daytime scenarios well. If you frequently drive in very dark conditions, verify the night vision specs before purchasing any dashcam.

Confirm Connectivity & Storage

WiFi connectivity (built into the Galphi) lets you pull footage directly to your smartphone without removing the SD card — a major convenience advantage. Ensure the dashcam supports a microSD card of at least 64GB (sold separately). Loop recording automatically overwrites old footage, so you never run out of space. For parking mode protection, budget an extra $15–$25 for a hardwire kit.
