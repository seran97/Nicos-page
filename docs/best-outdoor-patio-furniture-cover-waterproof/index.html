Buying Guide: 3 Steps to the Right Patio Cover

Measure Your Furniture Precisely

Before purchasing any outdoor patio furniture cover waterproof, measure your sofa's width, depth, and height. The Porch Shield cover is 77W x 35D x 35H inches — ideal for standard 3-seater sofas. A cover that's too loose will catch wind and blow off; too tight and it won't fit over cushions. Add 2–3 inches of clearance on each side for the best fit and airflow underneath.

Verify Waterproofing & UV Protection

Not all "waterproof" covers are created equal. Look for covers with a polyester fabric rated at 600D or higher, a PVC or polyurethane coating on the underside, and UV inhibitors woven into the material. The Porch Shield cover uses heavy-duty 600D Oxford polyester with a waterproof coating — verified by 928 reviewers across multiple climate zones including rainy Pacific Northwest and humid Southeast regions.

Check Securing Mechanisms

Wind is the #1 enemy of patio furniture covers. Prioritize covers with adjustable straps, buckle closures, or drawstring hems that cinch under the furniture legs. The Porch Shield cover includes windproof straps with buckles on both sides, keeping it secure even in gusty conditions. Avoid covers with only elastic hems — they tend to slip off during storms and provide minimal protection when you need it most.
