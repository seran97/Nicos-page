Buying Guide

What to Look For in a Gaming Headset

If you're new to picking a gaming headset, these are the details worth checking before you add anything to your cart.

Check the connector type firstConfirm whether your console generation needs a 3.5mm jack, USB dongle, or Bluetooth before buying — plenty of returns happen because of a mismatched connector. Cross-platform headsets like this one simplify things if you own more than one system.
Weigh comfort over spec sheetsClamping force and weight matter more day-to-day than frequency response numbers most buyers never test. Look for lightweight builds under 300g if you play in multi-hour sessions.
Read the mic reviews specificallyAudio quality on cheap headsets varies less than mic quality does — search reviews for mentions of static, tinny voice pickup, or teammates complaining. A clear unidirectional mic is worth prioritizing over marginal driver upgrades.
