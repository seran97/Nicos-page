Buying Guide

What to Look For in a Anti-Chafing Shorts For Women

A few details make the difference between shorts that actually stop chafing and ones that just look the part.

Prioritize inseam length over brand nameChafing happens where thighs rub, so the inseam length needs to extend past where your thighs naturally touch. Check the size chart's inseam measurement, not just the size label.
Choose fabric based on your primary useMoisture-wicking blends work better for workouts, while smoother, thinner fabrics are better suited for wearing under dresses without visible bulk.
Buy multi-packs for laundry-heavy usesIf you'll wear these daily under dresses or for exercise, a multi-pack avoids the situation where your one good pair is always in the wash.
