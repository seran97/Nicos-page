Buying Guide

What to Look For in a Car Jump Starter Portable

A jump starter is only useful if it's charged and rated for your vehicle, so check these details before buying.

Match battery capacity to your engine sizeLarger V6 or V8 engines need more cranking amps than a compact car. Check the device's peak amperage rating against your vehicle's battery requirements before relying on it in an emergency.
Keep it charged and check it periodicallyA jump starter with a dead battery is useless when you need it most. Set a reminder to recharge it every month or two, even if unused.
Verify the tire inflator's PSI range and speedCheck that the stated PSI range covers your tire's requirements, and note that portable inflators are generally slower than shop-grade compressors for large tires.
