Buying Guide: 3 Steps to the Right Solar Light

  1. Assess Your Coverage Area

    Measure the square footage you need to illuminate. A commercial-grade unit like the Deepn TW030 covers large driveways, parking areas, and commercial entrances. For areas over 500 sq ft, you'll want at least 5000W equivalent output — the TW030's 12000W rating gives you significant headroom and future-proofs your installation.

  2. Check Your Sunlight Hours

    Solar lights depend on daily sun exposure to charge. Most quality units need 6–8 hours of direct sunlight for a full night's operation. Use a sun-hours map for your ZIP code. If you average 5+ hours, the Deepn TW030 will perform at its rated capacity. In low-sun regions, consider supplemental lighting for critical areas.

  3. Verify Mounting & Motion Sensor Settings

    Before purchasing, confirm you have a suitable mounting surface — wall, pole, or eave. The TW030 includes adjustable mounting hardware. Also review the motion sensor sensitivity range (typically 10–16 ft) and ensure it aligns with your detection zone. Adjustable sensitivity prevents false triggers from passing vehicles or animals.
