Buying Guide

How to Choose the Right Mechanical Gaming Keyboard

Not sure if the ASUS ROG Strix Scope II X is right for you? Follow these three steps to make a confident decision.

Identify Your Layout Preference

Decide between full-size (100%), tenkeyless (TKL), or compact (60–75%) layouts. If you use a numpad regularly for work or gaming macros, a full-size keyboard like the ROG Strix Scope II X is the right choice. Compact boards save desk space but sacrifice key count.

Choose Your Switch Type

Mechanical switches come in three main flavors: linear (smooth, quiet — like the ROG NX Snow V2), tactile (bump feedback, no click), and clicky (audible click + bump). For fast-paced gaming, linear switches are widely preferred. The hot-swap feature on this keyboard means you can always swap to a different switch type later without soldering.

Evaluate Value vs. Budget

At $94.99, the ASUS ROG Strix Scope II X sits in the sweet spot — offering hot-swappable, pre-lubed switches and a full-size layout that typically cost $130+ on competing premium boards. If your budget is firm below $70, consider entry-level options, but expect to sacrifice hot-swap capability and switch quality.
